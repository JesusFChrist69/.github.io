Yume Nikki

So basically, I'm gonna be reviewing a game here. You've probably already read the first review, but this time I'm goinng to be way worse at it so... enjoy I guess. This is a sureal horror/exploration game that was pretty infulential in that area. It spawned a lot of games that had similar vibes that were simply called "yume nikki" games most of the time, which isn't the most creative title but who cares.


How it works/How you play it

So basically, when you first get into the game you're just in a bedroom full of kinda interesting stuff I guess. Just standard regular bedroom stuff and you actually start playing by going to sleep where you end up on the patio. From there you walk back into your bedroom to find it exactly the same exept this time you can go outside to find The Nexus. Connected to the nexus are 12 central worlds that all lead to other worlds that are connected to each other. You could go through one door and dozens of worlds and find yourself back at some other main world connected to the nexus. In each of these worlds are items called "effects" they change your appearence and some grant abilties. For example the bike effect just puts you on a bike and lets you move faster. To beat the game you need to collect them all, but not in any particular order

What is it?

This game is about... well I'm not quite sure but that's the point. See, unlike most games, there's not actually any liner progresion or almost any progresion at all. The only thing that changes from the start of the game to the end is how many effects you have and they aren't usually needed to move through the worlds. Yume Nikki is actually an exploration game centered around surealism that is always sureal, but sometimes also horrifiying. It's not focused on horror, but it's not afraid to put you in places that are rather... disturbing as you can see in the image I provided

Why do people like it?

So, there's of course people who like horror (myself included) but that's actually not all there is to it. There's also the sureal part. By that I mean that not all of it's sureal stuff is horror related. There's actually quite a few places in the game loved by the community for their beauty rather than their horror. So the game could make you say, "what the hell!" or it can make you go, "Oh my, that's great" within like 30 seconds of each other. The best example of that I saw while playing was The Sky Garden (that's where the picture is from). A place that's really nice looking connected to basically hell through a shed filled with white flowers. you get there by going up some random stairs in a desert wasteland and it's just a whirlwind of "what" to go through such different places within 2 feet of each other and that's great.
